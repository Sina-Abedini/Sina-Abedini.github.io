Project Information

  • Title: Expression and Purification of Prokaryotic CNNMs for Nanodisc Reconstitution
  • Supervisor: Dr. Kalle Gehring
  • Project Date: Sep 2023 - Present

Project Summary

The important role that membrane proteins (MPs) play in pharmacologically valuable cellular processes such as cellular signaling and membrane transport makes them desirable cases for structural studies for potential therapeutics. The inherent partial hydrophobicity, flexibility, and lack of stability of MPs makes them difficult to purify and challenging to crystallize. The ability of cryo-EM to overcome the challenges associated with other structural methods for MP studies allows researchers to examine them in a near-native state. For structural analysis of MPs by cryo-EM, they are either solubilized in the presence of detergents or detergent-like amphipols, or reconstituted in lipid nanodiscs.

Detergents are required to solubilize membrane proteins, but the molecules may also destabilize their structures. In addition, they may complicate the cryo-EM grid preparation process and may lower the contrast of cryo-EM images. A recently popularized method for studying membrane proteins with single-particle cryo-EM involves the use of lipid nanodiscs, which stabilize the proteins in a native-like lipid bilayer, preserving their structural and functional properties during analysis. These nanodiscs consist of a patch of lipid bilayer that is surrounded by a belt of membrane scaffolding proteins termed MSPs. Although nanodiscs are highly preferred for studying membrane protein structures, no universal method exists for their assembly.

The membrane scaffolding proteins often require optimization to ensure the MSP belt around the lipid forms correctly, and their successful formation is typically validated using size exclusion chromatography to assess size and homogeneity. The goal of this project is to express and purify the above-mentioned TtCorB and MSP proteins and screen for the optimal Protein:MSP:Lipid ratio for the proper reconstitution of the target MP into the nanodisc assembly for further structural studies.
