Presentation Summary

VEGF is a member of the PDGF/VEGF growth factor family. This factor induces proliferation and migration of vascular endothelial cells and is essential for both physiological and pathological angiogenesis. VEGF activity is not limited to the vascular system and it is known to play a role in numerous physiological functions such as bone formation, hematopoiesis, wound healing and development. The history of VEGF discovery along with the protein structure of the different classes of VEGF and multiple isoforms of VEGF that result from alternative mRNA splicing of its single 8-exon gene are covered in the first half of the presentation. The second half mainly focuses on the function and mechanism of action of the VEGF tyrosine kinase receptors and finally the roles of VEGF in the body and diseases such as Cancer and COVID-19 are discussed.
