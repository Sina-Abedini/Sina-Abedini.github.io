Project Information

  • Title: Fusion of an engineered thermostable variant of apocytochrome b562(BRIL) into target membrane protein
  • Supervisor: Dr. Kalle Gehring
  • Project Date: Sep 2023 - Present

Project Summary

CNNM/CorB proteins are a conserved family of integral membrane proteins associated with Mg2+ transport. Originally discovered in bacteria, there are four CNNM proteins in humans, which are involved in divalent cation transport and diseases such as hypomagnesemia, Jalili syndrome, and cancer. Insight into the structure of CNNMs will provide a framework for understanding their role in Mg2+ transport and mutations that result in diseases.

Currently, the transmembrane domain structure has only been captured in a single conformation, offering just one view of the entire transport process. Assuming CNNMs transport divalent cations by a rocker-switch mechanism, it will be necessary to obtain structures in multiple conformations, revealing different stages of the process to fully grasp the transport mechanism. The goal of this project is to use Cryo-EM SPA to elucidate the structure of the TtCorB membrane protein from a thermophilic, gram-negative bacterium (Tepidiphilus thermophilus) which shares 25% sequence identity to the conserved core of human CNNM2.

The main challenge is that high-resolution structure determination of small proteins (<100 KD) remains difficult using this technique. In addition, membrane proteins are usually embedded in detergent micelles or nanodiscs, making it even more difficult to accurately orient them. One solution to this problem is to use antibodies (Fabs) bound to nanobodies (NB) as fiducial marks to add mass to the target protein and assist in its orientation. This goal can be achieved by the fusion of the above-mentioned BRIL protein into loops connecting the Î±-helices of the membrane protein or its N- and C- termini. This BRIL insertion serves as a binding site for a NB-stabilized Fab in a plug-and-play fashion. The 12KD BRIL bound to the 50KD Fab and the additional MW added by the NB will add sufficient mass and asymmetry to the target protein to help facilitate a high-resolution structure determination.
