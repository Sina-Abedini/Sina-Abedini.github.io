Presentation Summary

CRISPR-Cas is an adaptive immune system in prokaryotes that since it’s discovery, has been developed into a powerful tool for genome editing and has proven to have diverse applications in the fields of Biotechnology, medicine and basic genetic research. This presentation begins with the discovery and evolution of CRISPR-Cas and then focuses on it’s mechanism of action in bacteria as a defense strategy against phage invaders. Several types of CRISPR-cas systems are introduced and their applications in genome editing, disease treatment, drug delivery and biotechnology are discussed. Finally, the ethical issues and moral dilemmas created with the advent of gene manipulation facilitated by CRISPR are debated
